Why It’s Not Among the Big Three

Hunter × Hunter is often called the "Uncrowned King" of shōnen anime. Despite its massive popularity and critical acclaim, it is not officially part of the Big Three. The term "Big Three" was coined in the 2000s to describe One Piece, Naruto, and Bleach—the three titles that dominated manga sales, anime broadcasting slots, and cultural influence at the same time.

While Hunter × Hunter shares many qualities with these giants—such as a deep, evolving plot, complex characters, and a unique power system called Nen—it never quite reached the same level of mainstream dominance due to its irregular publication schedule and frequent hiatuses.

Hunter × Hunter missed inclusion largely because of its irregular publication schedule. While beloved for its intricate storytelling, power system, and character depth, frequent hiatuses prevented it from maintaining the same continuous mainstream dominance.

However, its influence on the shōnen genre is undeniable, inspiring many subsequent series and earning a dedicated global fanbase. It is often praised for its mature themes, strategic battles, and emotional depth, setting it apart from its contemporaries.

Created by Yoshihiro Togashi, Hunter × Hunter follows Gon Freecss as he embarks on a journey to become a Hunter and find his father. The series is praised for its complex characters, moral ambiguity, and innovative battle strategies.

Its Unique Influence

- Introduced one of the most versatile and complex power systems: Nen.
- Balanced lighthearted adventure with dark, mature storytelling.
- Inspired countless other manga and anime, including direct influences on the Big Three themselves.
- Praised for deconstructing traditional shōnen tropes while still delivering emotional highs and thrilling battles.

Its influence is seen in many modern shōnen series that emphasize strategic battles and moral ambiguity.

Legacy

Even though it isn’t officially part of the Big Three, many fans and critics regard Hunter × Hunter as superior in writing and thematic depth. Its absence from the Big Three list isn’t due to lack of quality but rather timing and consistency of publication. For that reason, it proudly stands as the Uncrowned King.

Whether or not it’s included in the Big Three, Hunter × Hunter has undeniably left a lasting impact on the anime and manga world.

Its complex characters, intricate plotlines, and unique power system have earned it a dedicated fanbase and critical acclaim.

As of 2024, Hunter × Hunter continues to be celebrated for its storytelling and influence on the shōnen genre.

The series has been adapted into two successful anime adaptations, further cementing its place in anime history.

Its themes of friendship, ambition, and the pursuit of dreams resonate with audiences of all ages.

Hunter × Hunter has won numerous awards, including the Shogakukan Manga Award and the Kodansha Manga Award.

The series has been translated into multiple languages, making it accessible to a global audience.

Hunter × Hunter has a rich and diverse cast of characters, each with their own unique abilities and personalities.

Main Characters

- Gon Freecss: The optimistic and determined protagonist on a quest to find his father.
- Killua Zoldyck: A skilled assassin with a complex past, Gon’s best friend and ally.
- Kurapika: The last survivor of the Kurta Clan, driven by vengeance against the Phantom Troupe.
- Leorio Paradinight: A medical student with a strong sense of justice and loyalty to his friends.

Killua Zoldyck

Killua is one of the most complex and compelling characters in the series. As a member of the infamous Zoldyck family of assassins, he has been trained from a young age to be a ruthless killer.

However, despite his dark upbringing, Killua is also shown to have a kind and compassionate side, particularly towards his friends Gon, Kurapika, and Leorio. His character arc is one of the most dynamic in the series, as he struggles to reconcile his past as an assassin with his desire to live a normal life and form genuine connections with others. Throughout the series, Killua grapples with issues of identity, loyalty, and morality, often questioning the values and beliefs instilled in him by his family. His relationship with Gon is particularly significant, as it serves as a catalyst for much of his growth and development. Together, they embark on numerous adventures and face countless challenges, with Killua often acting as Gon's protector and confidant.

Overall, Killua's character arc is a central focus of Hunter × Hunter, and his growth and development are integral to the series' themes and narrative. He is a complex and multifaceted character who embodies many of the series' core themes, including friendship, loyalty, and the struggle for self-discovery.

Kurapika

Kurapika is a deeply driven and complex character whose story is marked by themes of vengeance, justice, and the struggle for identity.

As the last surviving member of the Kurta Clan, Kurapika's primary motivation is to avenge his fallen people by hunting down the Phantom Troupe, a group of notorious criminals responsible for their massacre. His quest for revenge is fueled by a deep sense of loss and a desire to restore honor to his clan. Throughout the series, Kurapika grapples with the moral implications of his actions, often questioning whether his pursuit of vengeance is justified or if it has consumed him entirely. His character arc is marked by moments of introspection and self-discovery, as he learns to balance his desire for justice with the need for compassion and understanding. Kurapika's relationships with other characters, particularly Gon and Killua, also play a significant role in his development, as they provide him with support and perspective on his journey.

Overall, Kurapika's character arc is a central focus of Hunter × Hunter, and his growth and development are integral to the series' themes and narrative. He is a complex and multifaceted character who embodies many of the series' core themes, including justice, identity, and the struggle for redemption.

Leorio Paradinight

Leorio is a character who provides both comic relief and emotional depth to the series. Initially introduced as a somewhat brash and self-centered individual, Leorio's true motivations and character depth are gradually revealed over the course of the story.

His primary goal is to become a doctor, driven by a desire to help others and make a positive impact on the world. This aspiration is rooted in his own experiences with loss and hardship, which have shaped his compassionate and empathetic nature. Throughout the series, Leorio's character arc is marked by moments of growth and self-discovery, as he learns to balance his ambitions with his loyalty to his friends and his sense of justice. His relationships with Gon, Killua, and Kurapika are particularly significant, as they provide him with support and camaraderie on their shared journey. Leorio's character also serves as a reminder of the importance of perseverance and determination in the face of adversity, as he overcomes numerous challenges and obstacles in pursuit of his dreams.

Overall, Leorio's character arc is an important aspect of Hunter × Hunter, and his growth and development contribute to the series' themes of friendship, ambition, and the pursuit of one's goals. He is a multifaceted character who embodies many of the series' core values, including compassion, loyalty, and resilience.

The Phantom Troupe

The Phantom Troupe, also known as the Spiders, is a notorious group of thieves and criminals who play a significant role in the series. Each member of the Troupe possesses unique abilities and personalities, making them a formidable and unpredictable force.

The Troupe is led by Chrollo Lucilfer, a charismatic and enigmatic figure who commands the loyalty and respect of his followers. The members of the Troupe are bound by a strong sense of camaraderie and loyalty to one another, often referring to each other as "brothers" or "sisters." Throughout the series, the Phantom Troupe serves as both antagonists and complex characters in their own right, with their motivations and backstories gradually revealed over time. Their actions often have significant consequences for the main characters, particularly Kurapika, who seeks revenge against them for the massacre of his clan. The Troupe's dynamic and interactions with one another also provide insight into themes of loyalty, friendship, and the blurred lines between good and evil.

Overall, the Phantom Troupe is a central element of Hunter × Hunter, and their presence adds depth and complexity to the series' narrative. They are multifaceted characters who embody many of the series' core themes, including loyalty, identity, and the consequences of one's actions.
